Blog Post #2

So I have decided to deep dive into CSS by searching on the internet "the most interesting CSS properties".
On CS-Tricks, I decided to go with font-smoothing for today's blog. This property controls how anti-aliasing
is applied to the font in question. The CS-Tricks website states:

    "The problem is that after the standard browser anti-aliasing your font may
    look slightly different from what you see in design software."
The site continues to talk about how to troubleshoot this problem between Webkit and Firefox. My interest
lies with anti-aliasing; it is a technique used to eliminate "jaggies" that appear in objects in PC games.
Jaggies are created when pixels on the screen have to be aligned point to point to create diagnol lines, which then
reveals their jagged edges.
